# Acme Robotics — Code of Conduct

The Acme Robotics Code of Conduct sets out the principles and standards of behavior expected from every employee, contractor, and officer of the company. By accepting employment with Acme Robotics, you agree to uphold these standards.

## Our Core Values

1. **Integrity** — We are honest with our customers, our colleagues, and ourselves.
2. **Safety First** — We design, build, and operate robots that are safe for humans to be near.
3. **Respect** — We treat everyone with dignity, regardless of role, background, or seniority.
4. **Accountability** — We take responsibility for our work, including its mistakes.

## Workplace Behavior

Acme Robotics is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and intimidation. Conduct that is unwelcome, offensive, or based on protected characteristics — including but not limited to race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin — is strictly prohibited. Any employee who experiences or witnesses such behavior is encouraged to report it through the confidential ethics hotline at `ethics@acme-robotics.internal` or via the anonymous portal.

Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is itself a serious violation of this code.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees must avoid situations in which their personal interests conflict — or appear to conflict — with the interests of Acme Robotics. Examples include:

- Holding a financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer.
- Accepting gifts of more than nominal value (USD 75) from a vendor.
- Hiring or directly managing a close family member without prior HR approval.

Any potential conflict must be disclosed in writing to HR within **5 business days** of becoming aware of it.

## Outside Employment

Employees may engage in outside work or consulting **only with prior written approval from their manager and HR**. Outside employment must not:

- Compete with Acme Robotics' business or products.
- Use Acme Robotics' confidential information, equipment, or facilities.
- Interfere with the employee's regular duties or working hours.

Teaching, writing, and unpaid volunteer work generally do not require approval, but should still be disclosed to your manager.

## Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

All non-public information learned during employment — including designs, source code, customer lists, pricing, and unannounced products — is confidential. Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of employment. Any invention or improvement created during employment, and related to Acme Robotics' business, is the property of the company.

## Use of Company Resources

Company laptops, accounts, and credentials are for business use. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work, consume significant resources, or violate any other policy.

## Reporting Violations

If you suspect a violation of this Code, please raise it with your manager, HR, or the ethics hotline. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Substantiated violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and potentially civil or criminal proceedings.
